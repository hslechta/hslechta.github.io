About Me

I was born and raised in Lancaster, Pennsylvania. My parents were both music teachers, so I grew up singing and playing instruments alongside them. By middle school, I was singing in the school chorus and playing percussion in the school band. Around the same time, my middle school gave us the option to study a foreign language. Because Lancaster is in an Amish (Pennsylvania Dutch/German) area, I chose to learn German. Once I made it into high school, I joined my high school's theater organization, marching band, jazz band, and a cappella choir. In my senior year, I served as the drum major of the marching band for a show based on the music of Queen, and I also became the president of the theater organization while performing as Oliver Warbucks in a production of Annie.

For my undergraduate degree, I went to Cornell University and took classes in a wide variety of fields including physics, computer science, math, music, history, archaeology, and linguistics. While there, I sang with the Cornell University Glee Club and played the Cornell Chimes. I graduated from Cornell with a BA in Linguistics, and then spent two years earning an MA in Linguistics from the University of Georgia. I am currently enrolled in an MS in Software Development program from Boston University.
